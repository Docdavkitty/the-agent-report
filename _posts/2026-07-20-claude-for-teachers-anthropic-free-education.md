## TL;DR

- **Free for verified K-12 educators** in the US, with premium Claude capabilities, Claude Code, and Cowork included
- **Standards-aligned** via Learning Commons connector — mapped to academic standards in all 50 states
- **AFT partnership** on privacy gold standards; data not used for training, FERPA-compliant
- **Open-source skills library** published on GitHub, plus free AI fluency course co-created with Teach for America
- **Sign-up deadline**: June 30, 2027 for one year of free access

## What Claude for Teachers Offers

Anthropic's newest education initiative, [announced July 14](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-teachers), gives verified K-12 teachers free access to Claude's premium tier. The package includes not just the chat interface, but also **Claude Code** and **Claude Cowork** — Anthropic's agentic coding and document-processing tools — which means teachers can automate lesson planning, differentiate materials for varied reading levels, and analyze class data without touching a spreadsheet.

The product connects to [Learning Commons](https://learningcommons.org), giving Claude direct access to academic standards across all 50 US states. Below each standard are the smaller competencies and the typical learning progression, so lesson plans are scaffolded, not generic. Curricular resources from OpenSciEd and Illustrative Mathematics are also integrated. *(Source: [EdSurge — Anthropic Introduces Claude for Teachers](https://www.edsurge.com/news/anthropic-introduces-claude-for-teachers))*

## Privacy and Trust

Anthropic is positioning privacy as a differentiator. **Training is off for verified teacher accounts**, and student data is protected under a K-12 Data Processing Addendum written to comply with FERPA. The company is working with the **American Federation of Teachers (AFT)** to develop industry-wide "Gold Standards" for K-12 AI safety and privacy.

> "We've been working with Anthropic on a Gold Standard that sets out industry best practices for safety and privacy in K-12 education," said Randi Weingarten, AFT President. "It's important that Anthropic is committing to these principles in their new Claude for Teachers." *(Source: [Anthropic Newsroom](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-teachers))*

## Skills Library and Open-Source Release

The teaching skills — co-developed with Learning Commons and tested with teachers at Prospect Schools in Brooklyn — have been released as an [open-source repository on GitHub](https://github.com/anthropics/k12-teacher-skills). Anthropic also published a technical write-up on how the skills were evaluated, aimed at other builders in the education space.

A free, model-agnostic **AI Fluency for K-12 Teachers** course, co-created with Teach for America, is available through Anthropic's learning platform. A train-the-trainer module was co-developed with the AFT.

## The Competitive Landscape

Claude for Teachers enters a crowded field. OpenAI's [ChatGPT for Teachers](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-for-teachers/), Microsoft's Elevate for Educators, and Google's AI Educator Series all compete for the same audience. Anthropic's differentiator is the combination of free premium-tier access (including agentic tools), standards alignment, and privacy commitments backed by union partnerships.

The initiative is part of Anthropic's broader [partnership with the Gates Foundation](https://www.anthropic.com/news/gates-foundation-partnership) to co-develop AI tools for K-12 education. A pilot evaluation is planned in the Detroit Public Schools Community District, studying impact on educator well-being and practice.

## Getting Access

US K-12 educators can verify their status at [claude.com/solutions/teachers](https://claude.com/solutions/teachers). Sign-up by June 30, 2027 grants a full year of free access. A dedicated offering for schools and districts is coming later.
