**TL;DR:** OpenAI's new paper *"The Shift to Agentic AI: Evidence from Codex"* documents an inflection point: 97.9% of OpenAI employees now use Codex, active users grew 5× in H1 2026, and non-developer usage exploded — 137× for individuals, 189× for organizations since August 2025. Co-authored with academics from Columbia, Wharton, and Duke, it argues agentic AI has crossed from curiosity into enterprise-wide transformation.

---

## Introduction

On June 25, 2026, OpenAI published *"The Shift to Agentic AI: Evidence from Codex"* *(Source: [arXiv — 2606.26959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26959))* — a data-rich study of how its Codex platform is reshaping work. Co-authored with researchers from Columbia, Wharton, and Duke, the paper uses internal telemetry to argue that 2026 marks the year agentic AI reached critical mass. Thomas Claburn's coverage for *The Register* captured the headline: nearly every OpenAI employee now relies on agents *(Source: [The Register — OpenAI says 97.9 percent of its employees are now using agents](https://www.theregister.com/2026/06/25/openai_codex_agent_adoption/))*.

## Key Findings

### Near-Universal Internal Adoption

OpenAI's workforce has gone all-in. Codex adoption rose from ~40% in August 2025 to **97.9%** by mid-2026 — a near-saturation rate signaling the tool is no longer optional but essential infrastructure.

### User Growth Across the Board

Active users grew **5×** in H1 2026. More striking is where growth comes from: **non-developer usage** surged 137× for individuals and 189× for organizations since August 2025, reflecting adoption far beyond engineering into legal, operations, marketing, and HR.

### Intensity of Use Is Accelerating

It's not just more users — they're using Codex more deeply. The share of users submitting **8+ hour tasks** increased nearly 10× since the start of 2026, signaling growing trust in agents for long-running autonomous work. The median legal-role user now generates **13× more monthly output tokens** than in November 2025.

## What This Means

The data points to a genuine tipping point. When 97.9% of a company's employees use agents and non-technical roles generate 13× more output than six months prior, agentic AI is no longer an experiment — it's the default mode of knowledge work. The academic collaboration lends independent credibility to what might otherwise read as a vendor case study. Organizations on the sidelines should treat this as a wake-up call: the shift isn't coming — it already happened.

## FAQ

**Is Codex just for developers?** No. The most dramatic growth — 137× individuals, 189× organizations — comes from non-developer roles. Legal, marketing, and operations are the fastest-growing segments.

**What is an "8+ hour task"?** A complex, multi-step objective delegated to Codex that runs autonomously for eight or more hours. The 10× increase shows users trust agents with high-stakes, long-duration work.

**How reliable is internal data?** The paper uses telemetry — actual token counts, session durations, task metadata — not surveys. External academic co-authors from Columbia, Wharton, and Duke add rigor.

**Where can I read the full paper?** On [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26959) and the [OpenAI blog](https://openai.com/index/how-agents-are-transforming-work/). The PDF is at OpenAI's CDN.
